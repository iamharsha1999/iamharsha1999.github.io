Speed Dating
Harshanand B A, David Shimshoni, Bruno Fernandes, Claire Saint-Donat, Nikki Gharachorloo

What influences love at first sight? (Or, at least, love in the first four minutes?) This project utilises the dataset compiled by Columbia Business School professors Ray Fisman and Sheena Iyengar for their paper Gender Differences in Mate Selection: Evidence From a Speed Dating Experiment. The purpose of the study is to find the important factors that affect love at first sight. As a part of this project we tried to answer three important questions:

  1. Are people more likely to match with their first or last date of the evening ? Is there a difference in the rate at which men decide they want a second date as opposed to women ?
  2. What attributes are more correlated with one partner matching with another?
  3. Can we reasonably predict the likelihood of a date ending in a match using attributes provided

Covid-19-Bot
Harshanand B A

Programmed a python bot to fetch daily stats of covid-19 in India and do an EDA using Pandas, Seaborn. The bot also generates a state wise heatmap containing details such as confirmed cases, fatalities, number of tests etc. This heat map was generated an Image with details in 8 different regional languages of the country and posted to language specific Instagram pages. The instagram page garnered more than 5000 followers within a week for each page.
